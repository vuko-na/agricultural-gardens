Green Sky Rooftop Garden – Johannesburg

Description:

The Green Sky Rooftop Garden is a pioneering urban agriculture project established atop a commercial building in Johannesburg by local entrepreneur and urban farmer, Mapaseka Dlamini. Recognized as one of the largest rooftop farms in South Africa, it transforms previously unused space into a lush green haven, cultivating more than 3,000 gourmet lettuce and basil plants. The garden is a symbol of innovation, sustainability, and community empowerment, operating as both a functional farm and a model for urban food production in high-density cities.

Urban Rooftop Garden.

Crops Grown:

Gourmet lettuce, basil.

  • Inspiration & Awareness

Urban Agriculture Initiative – Johannesburg

Description:

The Urban Agriculture Initiative (UAI) is a transformative program launched in Johannesburg to address urban food insecurity and empower communities through localized food production. Initiated in October 2017, the project started modestly with a single rooftop garden and has since grown into a broader network of urban farms that utilize underused spaces—such as rooftops and vacant lots—to grow fresh, organic produce right in the heart of the city. The initiative is a collaboration between urban farmers, local NGOs, and private-sector partners, all working together to make sustainable food systems a reality in densely populated areas. Today, UAI farms supply fresh ingredients like spinach, basil, lettuce, chives, and edible flowers to restaurants, cafes, and local markets throughout Johannesburg.

Type:

Urban Community Gardens

Crops Grown:

Spinach, basil, lettuce, chives, edible flowers.

Challenges:

  • Space Constraints in Urban Environments
  • High Start-Up Costs
  • Lack of Agricultural Knowledge
  • Policy and Regulation Barriers

Solutions & Innovations:

  • Rooftop Utilization
  • Modular and Scalable Systems
  • Community Training Programs
  • Sustainable Partnerships

Impact:

  • Improved Urban Food Security
  • Job Creation & Skills Development
  • Sustainable Urban Development
  • Strengthened Local Economies
  • Scalable Urban Farming Model

Recommendations:

iGardi Project – Langa, Cape Town

Description:

The iGardi Project at Thembani Primary School in Langa, Cape Town, is a transformative educational and nutritional program established through the nonprofit organization Call 2 Care. Designed to be more than just a garden, the initiative integrates firsthand agricultural learning with the school’s existing curriculum, giving young learners the opportunity to cultivate, maintain, and learn from a fully functional vegetable garden. The project aims to foster an early understanding of sustainability, environmental stewardship, and food security. Students participate in eco-lessons, where they learn about soil health, planting cycles, composting, and water conservation—all while growing a range of climate-appropriate vegetables. These lessons are woven into the broader goal of building healthier communities through education, empowerment, and environmental awareness.

Mamelodi Campus Garden

Description:

The Mamelodi Campus Food Garden is a collaborative, community-focused initiative launched by the University of Pretoria to address food insecurity, support local development, and provide experiential learning for students. Situated within the Mamelodi township—an area facing prominent levels of poverty and limited food access—the garden was initiated by a group of staff and student volunteers as both a training site and a source of fresh, nutritious produce. The garden is cultivated using eco-friendly, low-cost techniques, growing a diverse selection of vegetables including Swiss chard, Chinese spinach, bell peppers, beetroot, spring onions, Brussels sprouts, and coriander. More than just a food source, the garden operates as an agro-educational space where students, residents, and early childhood educators learn about sustainable food systems, soil health, and organic farming practices.

Voiceout Deaf Collective

Description:

The Voiceout Deaf Farming Collective is a ground-breaking social enterprise based in Westonaria Agricultural Park, designed to empower members of the deaf community through inclusive, skill-based farming. Founded by Matebogo Victoria, the project provides a safe and productive environment where deaf individuals can learn agricultural practices, gain employment, and build a sense of community and purpose. Unlike traditional farms, Voiceout’s core principle is accessibility and inclusivity—from training methods to daily operations, everything is tailored to accommodate South African Sign Language (SASL), and the unique communication needs of deaf individuals. The collective cultivates high-demand crops like tomatoes and lettuce, which are distributed to local supermarkets and national chain stores, proving that social impact can go together with commercial viability.

Type:

Community Garden

Crops Grown:

Tomatoes and lettuce

Challenges:

  • Communication Barriers
  • Limited Educational Access
  • Social Exclusion & Stigma
  • Market Entry & Visibility

Solutions & Innovations:

  • Sign Language Integration in All Operations
  • Tailored Agricultural Training Programs

  • Strategic Partnerships with Retailers

Impact:

  • Skills Development & Empowerment

  • Community Transformation
  • Food Security & Local Supply
  • Replicable Social Enterprise Model

Recommendations:

INMED SEEDS FOR LIFE

Description:

The SEEDS FOR LIFE Campaign is a community-based food security initiative launched by INMED South Africa, a non-profit organization dedicated to health, nutrition, and sustainable livelihoods. The campaign emerged in direct response to the heightened poverty, hunger, and unemployment caused by the COVID-19 pandemic and broader economic challenges in South Africa. The goal of SEEDS FOR LIFE is to empower vulnerable households—particularly those led by women, elderly individuals, and people with disabilities—to become self-reliant by establishing small-scale home gardens. These gardens are tailored to local conditions and family preferences, enabling families to grow nutrient-dense vegetables like spinach, carrots, tomatoes, and cabbage right in their backyards. The campaign goes beyond just seed distribution by offering training, mentorship, and ongoing support, helping participants not only grow food, but also build confidence, resilience, and community cohesion.

EduPlant Programme

Description:

The EduPlant Programme is a long-standing, award-winning initiative that uses permaculture-based food gardens to tackle hunger, improve education, and strengthen communities across under-resourced areas in South Africa. Launched and managed by Food & Trees for Africa (FTFA), the program equips schools and their surrounding communities with the knowledge, tools, and support needed to grow their own nutritious food sustainably. EduPlant creates school-linked food security clusters, which consist of a network of schools working together, supported by a central training school and community mentors. These gardens serve as both learning environments and food production hubs, enhancing classroom learning while directly addressing malnutrition and poverty. Using the principles of permaculture, the gardens are designed to be self-sustaining, ecologically harmonious, and productive all year round. The program spans both rural and urban settings, adapting to local climate conditions and community dynamics.

School and Community Gardens.

Crops Grown:

Diverse range of vegetables and fruits.
